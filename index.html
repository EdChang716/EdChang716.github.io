Predicting Connection Strength in Dyadic Interactions via Verbal and Nonverbal Cues

Exploring the Anna Karenina Principle in Mental Representations of Friendships and Well-being

  • Used computational methodologies to examine the psychological and social dimensions linking mental representations of friendships to subjective well-being
  • Implemented Intersubject Correlation (ISC), mixed-effects modeling, and factor analysis in Python and R; finding: individuals with higher well-being show more homogeneous friendship representations within their networks (β = .133, p = .007)
  • Presented at the 2024 Taiwanese Psychological Association Annual Conference; manuscript in preparation for Nature Communications
  • Co-authored a manuscript as the first author, currently in prep. for submission

Cross-domain Text Semantic Similarity with Large Language Models

  • Investigated how well LLM-derived text embeddings capture cross-domain semantic similarity and how text length biases similarity estimates.
  • Built a Mandarin news corpus via Selenium + BeautifulSoup, labeled by domain and length; cleaned/standardized with pandas.
  • Extracted text embeddings and computed cosine similarities; evaluated clustering/retrieval and ran statistical controls, showing length significantly affects measured similarity (p < .001).
  • Compared GPT- and BERT-based models on classification/retrieval tasks and used statistical analyses to examine the impact of text length on similarity estimates.
  • Presented at the 2023 Taiwanese Psychological Association Annual Conference.

Research Project

Leveraging Deep Learning and Imputation Techniques for Accurate Water Quality Predictions: Findings from Taipei Bridge

  • Proposed an advanced water-quality prediction framework that integrates MICE-RF imputation and an LSTM-Encoder–Decoder (LSTM-ED) to improve forecasts of dissolved oxygen and ammonia nitrogen; awarded the College Student Research Scholarship (National Science and Technology Council).
  • Scraped and consolidated open water-quality records; applied multivariate imputation by chained equations with Random Forest (MICE-RF) to fill missing values and benchmarked against KNN imputation.
  • Built a multivariate, multi-step LSTM-ED model achieving an R² close to 0.9 for the next-step forecast, outperforming baseline LSTM and CNN-LSTM models.
